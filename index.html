We had a long day ahead of us so we started early in the morning. A ground transport vehicle (GTV) came to pick us for breakfast. That's me with my back to the camera stepping into one of the many GTV we would use that day.

    During breakfast they gave us our itinerary.
  1. Practice collecting a soil sample
  2. Liftoff – 0900 Hours
  3. Lunch on the moon
  4. Collect a soil sample
  5. Salute American Flag for PR photo
  6. Return home

Collecting a soil sample – Aldrin and I practicing our surface activities. I know it's hard to tell, but that's me on the left.

Apollo shuttle selfie.
0900 Hours. Liftoff!
If it's not on board it's to late now. I was thinking of my chocolate-milk I left sitting in the Mission Control breifing room.

Mission conrtrol anxiously prays for a flawless liftoff.

LIFTOFF! We're doing it!

First things first, lunch. We had some nuts, cheese sandwiches, and freeze dried icecream. Sadly no cholcolate-milk this trip.

I am using fancy equipment to collect soil from the moon's surface. I still think a shovel would have been easier. Check out our awsome landing craft in the background.

Photo Op! This one will be given to the newspaper for the front page.

It's hard to tell but I am definiatly smiling for the camera. Soil sample loaded and ready to return home.

Niel, Micheal, and I relaxing after a sucessful mission. I'm the one in the forground – Finially enjoying that chocolate milk.
I've only been back a short time but I'm already thinking about the next mission.
